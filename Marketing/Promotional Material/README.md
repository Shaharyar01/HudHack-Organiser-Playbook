## 🎨 Design & Assets
* **Tool of Choice:** We used **Canva** to design all of our promotional materials, from social media posts to physical flyers and digital screen graphics.
* **The Golden Rule:** Every poster and graphic featured a **massive, clear QR code** that linked directly to our pre-event website (where the sign-up form was located). If the QR code is too small to scan as someone walks past, it won't work.
* 📂 **Included in this folder:** We have provided all the original posters, digital screen graphics, and assets we used. Feel free to use them as inspiration or templates for next year!

## 📍 Physical & On-Campus Marketing
We made sure our branding was highly visible in high-traffic, relevant areas on campus—specifically the **SJ Building** and the **HA Building (Haslett)**, as HA is the main hub for computing students.

* **Printed Posters:** Strategically placed on physical noticeboards around both buildings.
* **Digital Screens:** We adapted our posters to fit the digital monitors around the SJ and HA buildings. 
  * ⚠️ **Crucial Note for Next Year:** *You will need to ask permission from the building administration or university IT staff to get your graphics added to the digital screen rotation! Do this early.*

## 🗣️ Face-to-Face Outreach (Highly Effective)
You cannot rely on posters alone. Getting out there and personally inviting people was one of our highest-converting tactics:
* **Lecture Shoutouts:** We coordinated with lecturers to do quick 2-3 minute pitches at the start or end of relevant classes. This gave us a captive audience of our exact target demographic.
* **Coffee Mornings:** We attended university coffee mornings to talk to students directly, answer their questions (e.g., "Do I need to be a pro coder?"), and encourage them to scan the QR code right then and there.

## 💻 Digital Marketing
* **Discord:** We heavily utilized the UoH Cyber Security Society Discord server. We dropped announcements, built hype with the theme reveal, and pinged `@everyone` to remind them to sign up.
* **Email Campaigns:** We sent out targeted email blasts to remind students to officially sign up before the deadline. 

## 💡 Advice for 2027
* **Keep doing lecture pitches!** Speaking directly to students in the room builds trust and removes the intimidation factor of attending a hackathon.
* **Over-communicate:** Students ignore a lot of emails. Sending follow-up reminders is crucial to making sure people don't just look at the site and forget to register.

The Theme
Break
The Loop
The "Break The Loop" challenge invites you to identify everyday frustrations or problems — whether university-specific or general issues in daily life — and use technology to design practical solutions that make real improvements. How many times have you encountered a problem and hoped someone else would fix it? This is your invitation to be that person.
Accessibility Sustainability Wellbeing Safety Support
4
Main Challenge
🎯 Your Mission
Identify a real problem.
Build a solution that breaks the cycle.
Think about the frustrations, inefficiencies, or inequalities you or others experience every day — at university, at home, in your community. Your job is to pick one of those problems and build a working tech solution that genuinely addresses it. It doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be real, relevant, and impactful.

The best projects will be ones that could make someone's life measurably better. There are no restrictions on which problem you choose — if it's a genuine loop worth breaking, it's fair game.
5
What to Build

You can build anything that solves the problem — there's no single right answer. Below are some formats to inspire you. Pick the one that best fits your idea and team's skills.

🌐
Website / Web App
A browser-based platform users can visit and interact with. Great for dashboards, information tools, or community platforms.
React HTML/CSS Next.js
📱
Mobile App
A smartphone app that users carry with them. Ideal for on-the-go tools, alerts, or wellbeing check-ins.
Flutter React Native Swift
🤖
AI / Chatbot Tool
An intelligent assistant or automated system that helps people get answers, support, or guidance faster.
OpenAI API LangChain Gemini
🔧
CLI / Script / Automation
A command-line tool, script, or automated pipeline that removes a repetitive or manual task from someone's workflow.
Python Bash Node.js
🔌
API / Backend Service
A data service or API that other apps could plug into. Powerful for sharing data, integrating systems, or enabling analysis.
FastAPI Express Flask
💡
Hardware / IoT Prototype
A physical or sensor-based solution — think smart alerts, environmental monitors, or accessibility devices.
Raspberry Pi Arduino Sensors
💡 Not sure what to build? Start with the problem, not the technology. Describe the frustration you want to fix in one sentence, then decide which format above would best solve it. Judges care more about impact than stack complexity.

How to Submit on Devpost
Deadline
Submit by 16:45 — before judging begins
All projects must be submitted on Devpost before the final sprint ends. Late submissions may not be considered.

hudhack2026.devpost.com
1
Go to the HUDHACK 2026 Devpost page
Visit hudhack2026.devpost.com or scan the link in #hudhack-2026 on Discord. Click Submit Project.
2
Create or log in to your Devpost account
Sign up at devpost.com if you don't already have an account. Only one team member needs to submit on behalf of the whole team.
3
Fill in your project details
Add your project name, a short description of what it does and the problem it solves, and the technologies used. Keep it clear and concise — judges will read this.
4
Add your project links
Include links to your GitHub repo, a live demo (if applicable), and any other relevant URLs. Make sure your repo is public before submitting.
5
Add your team members
Search for your teammates by their Devpost username or email and add them to the submission so everyone gets credit.
6
Click "Submit" — you're done!
Double-check everything looks correct, then hit Submit Project. You can edit your submission up until the deadline, so don't stress about it being perfect first time.
Tips for a strong submission: Write your description as if explaining to someone who hasn't seen your demo. Mention the problem, your solution, and any challenges you overcame. Screenshots or a short demo video go a long way!

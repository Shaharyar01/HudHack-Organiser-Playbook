🚀 The Future of HudHack: Next Year's Blueprint

HudHack 2026 was a massive success, especially considering the Cyber Security Society was officially launched this same year. Because we were building the entire organization and the event from scratch simultaneously, the core founding team had to wear every hat—from coding the infrastructure and designing the CTF to running the floor logistics on the day.

To make the job easier for future committees and to scale HudHack to its full potential, we have documented our core lessons learned. If you are taking the lead on the next event, here is a detailed operational roadmap of exactly what we recommend doing.

👥 Core Committee Structure and Delegation

The single most critical takeaway from this year is that you must delegate. As a founding team, the President and Vice President ended up covering almost all aspects of the event simply to get it off the ground. This is not sustainable for a larger event. You need to establish a strict, specialized team structure immediately upon taking office.

Appoint a dedicated Marketing Lead to oversee the entire promotional strategy and timeline, ensuring that outreach doesn't fall behind while technical planning is happening. Alongside them, assign a Social Media Manager who is solely responsible for running the accounts, engaging with partners online, and building consistent digital hype leading up to the event.

You must also recruit a Design & Branding Lead. Having one dedicated person to create logos, slide decks, social media assets, and promotional materials ensures brand consistency and removes a massive time sink from the core organizers. Finally, appoint a Treasurer whose entire focus is handling the society budget, executing sponsor outreach, and securing prize funding well in advance.

📅 Expanding the Format and Scale

To elevate HudHack to the level of top-tier university hackathons, the event needs to shift from a single-day sprint to a full 2-day weekend format. This is how standard hackathons operate across the country. A weekend timeline removes clashes with weekday lectures, allows significantly more students to attend, and gives teams the necessary hours to build, test, and deploy far more complex and impressive projects.

Furthermore, it is time to open the doors to external students. Allowing hackers from other universities to register and compete will drastically expand the talent pool, elevate the standard of the projects, and increase the overall prestige of the event. It shifts HudHack from an internal department activity to a recognized fixture in the UK student tech calendar.

🤝 Strategic Partnerships and Sponsorships

Scaling the event requires scaling the backing. Your first major partnership target should be Major League Hacking (MLH). Partnering with MLH provides immense credibility, access to hardware labs, and vital organizational resources. Keep in mind that MLH requires events to be open to external students, which perfectly aligns with the expansion goal mentioned above.

You should also engage Hackathons UK immediately. Do not wait until the middle of your planning phase; bring them into the conversation from day one to maximize their guidance and support network.

When it comes to funding, change the outreach strategy. Target companies that already have established relationships and partnerships with the university. Instead of just asking for general funds, invite them to sponsor specific tech challenges tied to the hackathon's theme and have them provide their own dedicated prizes.

Finally, think beyond the hackathon. Work to secure a Year-Round Society Sponsor. Instead of just seeking event-based funding that dries up after the hackathon, secure a corporate sponsor for the Cyber Security Society as a whole. This unlocks a consistent, reliable budget that allows you to run smaller, engaging mini-events throughout the entire academic year, keeping momentum high.

📢 The Marketing Pipeline

A larger, 2-day event requires a significantly longer marketing runway. You must start your promotions immediately upon returning for the academic year. Begin teasing the event, reaching out to your sponsor pipeline, and opening early-bird registrations much sooner than we did this year. Building early momentum and securing a solid base of registered attendees early on is the absolute key to successfully filling a weekend-long event.

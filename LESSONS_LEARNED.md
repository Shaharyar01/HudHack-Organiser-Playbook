# 🧠 HudHack 2026: Post-Mortem & Lessons Learned

This document is an honest look at how HudHack 2026 went. It covers what worked, what failed, and the critical logistical steps you need to tackle first. If you are organizing the next event, read this before you do anything else.

---

## 👥 1. Team Structure & Delegation
One of the most important things to do early on is properly structure your committee. Do not try to have everyone do everything.
* **Appoint Lead Organisers:** You need 1 or 2 people steering the ship, making final decisions, and keeping everyone on track.
* **Split into Dedicated Teams:** Divide the rest of the committee into specific teams so everyone knows their exact responsibilities. For example:
  * **Marketing Team:** Handles social media, lecture shoutouts, making the Canva assets, and Discord hype.
  * **CTF/Tech Team:** Builds the challenges, sets up Devpost, manages the website, and handles the hosting.
  * **Logistics Team:** Handles the room booking, food ordering, and SU paperwork.

---

## 🚨 2. The Absolute First Steps (Do these immediately)
Before you build a website or design a logo, you need to handle the bureaucracy and foundational partnerships.

* **Partner with Hackathons UK:** This is the first main thing you should do. Reach out to them early and remind them that we have worked with them before. They will provide templates, advice on challenges, theme ideas, and massive logistical support.
* **Sort out the SU Paperwork:** The Student Union requires a lot of paperwork and logistics for the event to happen. 
    * Fill out any SU forms for funding as your very first step.
    * **Level 2 Food Hygiene:** At least one committee member *must* get a Level 2 Food Hygiene Certificate if you are providing food. The SU will absolutely not approve the event without this. Get this done early.
    * **Attendance Tracking:** You have to take an official attendance register on the day and send it back to the SU after the event wraps up.

---

## 🤝 3. Sponsorships & Judging
* **Get Sponsors on Board Early:** Reach out to companies months in advance. Sponsors might want to set their own specific challenges or provide their own API prizes for the hackers, and that takes time to coordinate.
* **Confirm Your Judges:** Lock in your judges early, but always make sure you have 1 or 2 backup judges ready to step in just in case someone drops out at the last minute.

---

## 🌟 4. What Went Well (Keep doing this!)
* **The CTF Track:** The CTFs were way more popular than we expected. It gave everyone something to do, even if their main project broke. Make sure to make more (and harder) ones next time.
* **Face-to-Face Marketing:** Lecture shoutouts and talking to students directly at coffee mornings worked much better than just sending emails.
* **Roaming Judges:** Judging at the tables instead of bringing teams up on a stage saved us hours and kept the energy high.
* **Discord QR Codes:** Putting the QR codes directly on the tables meant everyone joined the server immediately.

---

## ⚠️ 5. What We Need to Change for Next Year
* **The Email Spam Filter:** Because we used the external society Gmail account, the university firewall sent our acceptance emails to spam. Next year, the ticketing script *must* be run from an official student email account (`@unimail.hud.ac.uk`).
* **Dedicated Discord Announcer:** We needed more frequent updates on Discord throughout the day (food arrivals, time remaining, etc.). Next year, assign a dedicated person on the marketing team whose *only* job on the day is making Discord announcements and answering questions.
* **Improve the Closing Ceremony:** We need to refine the flow of the closing ceremony to make it a better experience for the hackers and the sponsors handing out the awards. Plan the script and AV setup better.
